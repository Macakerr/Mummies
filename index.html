I. How to Make a Mummy

Ancient Egyptians didn't make mummies to be scary. They did it because they loved life! They believed that if you preserved your body, your spirit (called the Ka) could live forever in a paradise called the "Field of Reeds."

Step 1: The Salt Bath

To stop the body from decaying, priests used a special salt called Natron. It‚Äôs like a mix of baking soda and salt. They covered the body in it for 40 days to dry it out, kind of like making beef jerky!

Step 2: The Wrapping

Next, they wrapped the body in linen bandages. This wasn't random‚Äîit was an art! They placed Amulets (good luck charms) inside the layers to protect the person from bad spirits on their journey.

III. The Gods: Ancient Superheroes

The Egyptians had hundreds of gods. They weren't just statues; they were like family members who helped with different problems.

Anubis: The Guide

Anubis has the head of a Jackal (a wild dog). He isn't mean; he's actually a helper! He holds your hand and guides you through the dark Underworld so you don't get lost.

Bastet: The Cat Protector

Bastet has the head of a cat. Egyptians loved cats because they caught mice and snakes. Bastet was the goddess of home and happiness. If you had a cat, you were safe!

IV. Life in Ancient Egypt

They were just like us! Here are some fun facts about real people:

  • Cool Makeup: Everyone wore thick black eyeliner (Kohl). It wasn't just for fashion‚Äîit protected their eyes from the hot sun!
  • Pet Lovers: They loved animals. Some Pharaohs even had pet monkeys and cheetahs!
  • Board Games: Their favorite game was called Senet. It was a bit like "Sorry!" or "Chutes and Ladders."
